Banger combo for the agentic world imo: DDD + TDD + Gherkin defined user stories / features.

It makes a lot of sense when you think about it. This brings me back 15 years ago, when I was heavily using DDD + TDD and Gherkin to build heavy cloud based payment infrastructure in production, at scale, used by banks to process millions of tokenized cards payments (yeah tokenization existing in payment industry before crypto lol). 

It was a micro-services infrastructure, dealing with extremely sensitive data, performing mission critical tasks, and compliant with PCI-DSS and other payments standards / certifications.

The more I think about it, the more I think that this is exactly the kind of architecture that can shine today in the agentic world.

It can drastically help on the 2 most critical dimensions we have right now in the agentic world, now that basically the actual writing of the code is solved:
- specification / planning of the work
- validation of the outcome

Those are absolutely critical in order to get the most out of the models with agentic programming / workflows. If you are bad on those dimensions, there is no way you can truly leverage the agentic power. 

In fact, without being good on those elements, it can often lead to net negative outcome, requiring way too much handholding and human gating.

And it's not only that, having DDD and micro services (or modularity in general) totally makes sense in the agentic world. To avoid contamination effects, to reduce context pollution / bloating, to facilitate parallelization of the work with multiple agents, etc.

There was a famous saying in the micro services world, which was something like: "a good size of a micro service is a codebase that can fit in the head of a SINGLE developer". 

I wonder if this will translate to something like: "a good size of a micro service / module is a codebase that can fit in a SINGLE context window".
