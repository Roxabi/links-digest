RISK MANAGEMENT (THIS SAVES YOUR LIFE)

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% per trade. Ever.

> $100 account = $1 max risk per trade
> $1,000 account = $10 max risk
> $10,000 account = $100 max risk

Why? Because losing streaks happen.

If you risk 10% per trade, ten losses = game over. Risk 1%? Ten losses = down 10%. Painful but survivable.

Position Sizing Formula

Before entering any trade:

(Account size × 1%) ÷ (Entry price - Stop loss) = Position size

Example: $1,000 account, 1% risk = $10. Stop loss is 50 pips away.

$10 ÷ 50 pips = $0.20 per pip = 0.02 lots in Forex.

Stop Loss (Non-Negotiable)

Always set stop loss before entering. Not after. Before.

Once you're in and it's going against you, emotions will convince you to move it further away "to give it room."

That's how $10 losses become $200 losses.

Number one reason traders blow up: no stop loss or risking too much per trade.

Fix this, you're ahead of 80% of people.

BUILD YOUR EDGE

An edge isn't some secret strategy. It's a setup that wins more than it loses when you account for risk and reward.

55% win rate with 2:1 risk-to-reward = profitable. Math says so.

How to Find It

Pick one simple strategy. Support/resistance. Breakouts. Moving average crossovers. Whatever.

Don't combine fifteen indicators. That's just confusion.

Backtest it:

> Use TradingView replay mode (free)
> Go back in time, trade your strategy on historical data
> Take 100 trades
> Track every one

What's your win rate? Average winner vs average loser?

Net positive after 100 trades with consistent execution?

You might have an edge.

You Don't Need 90% Win Rate

40% win rate with 3:1 risk-to-reward makes you profitable.

You lose more often than you win but winners are big enough.

Example edge:

> Only trade first hour after open
> Only trade with the trend
> Only enter at key levels

Simple. Repeatable. Testable.

YOUR DAILY ROUTINE

Professional traders have a routine. Gamblers "trade" when they feel like it.

Pre-Market (15 minutes)

> Check economic calendar (Forex Factory, free)
> Mark key levels on charts
> Set max loss for the day ($30 on $1,000 account)
> Write it down

During Market

Only take trades matching your plan. That's it.

Your edge is breakouts but you see a perfect mean reversion? Don't take it.

Not your system.

Hit daily loss limit? Close platform. Immediately.

No "just one more trade to get back to breakeven."

That's how bad days become destroyed accounts.

Post-Market (10 minutes)

Journal. Screenshot trades. Write what you did, how you felt, what you learned.

Non-negotiable if you want to improve.

This routine is boring.

But boring and consistent makes money.

Exciting and impulsive makes you broke.
