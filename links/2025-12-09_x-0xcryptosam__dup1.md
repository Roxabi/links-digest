Neobanks disrupted the financial sector by removing physical branches and prioritizing a superior mobile-first user experience. Their rise was fueled by consumer demand for lower fees, instant access, and greater convenience. As stablecoin adoption continues to accelerate, the market is signaling an opportunity for the next iteration of this model in the form of a dedicated crypto neobank.

Centralized exchanges (CEXs) are the most common crypto neobanks today. They serve as the primary gateway to the crypto ecosystem by operating the most popular fiat on and offramps. They also hold the majority of circulating stablecoins. CEXs, which may seem like a new type of bank, are restricted by the same legacy financial rails as traditional neobanks because of their custodial nature. This dependence means they cannot offer the transparency, composability, or autonomy of DeFi

Today, no end-to-end crypto neobank exists outside traditional rails while offering the full suite of services users expect from their bank. To achieve a banking experience that is truly interoperable with the crypto ecosystem, we must look beyond centralized entities. I believe the answer lies in a DefiBank, a proposed model that reconstructs banking services using decentralized infrastructure to offer a purely onchain financial life cycle.

Save

Traditional Banks: Operate on a fractional reserve model where deposits are liabilities (IOUs) rather than user-owned assets. They lend out user funds to generate revenue, keeping the majority of the yield.

Neobanks: Function as technology wrappers for partner banks, meaning they are still bound by the fractional reserve model. High yields are often marketing subsidies rather than structural improvements.

DefiBanks: Use a self-custodial model where users hold bearer assets (like stablecoins) backed 1:1. "Save" is treated as a loss leader; treasury yields are passed through to users to attract the liquidity needed for profitable trading verticals.

Spend

Traditional Banks: Rely on a complex chain of intermediaries (issuing banks, card networks, processors) that extract significant fees, roughly 2-3% on any purchase.

Neobanks: Typically issue prepaid debit cards because they lack a balance sheet for credit. They collect interchange fees and split them with their partner banks.

DefiBanks: Enable wallet-to-wallet payments that bypass card networks entirely, reducing fees to just the cost of gas. This allows them to capture the entire payments stack and potentially offer reward-bearing stablecoins to merchants.

Earn

Traditional Banks: Historically silo savings and investment products, relying on the spread (NIM) between what they pay depositors and what they earn on loans.

Neobanks: Disrupted the silo model by integrating trading directly alongside savings. However, "free" trading is often monetized through Payment for Order Flow (PFOF) or hidden spreads.

DefiBanks: Generate high-margin revenue through trading fees and preventing MEV (Maximal Extractable Value); a hidden tax on onchain activity. They act as neutral routers, finding the best prices across DEXs while protecting users from slippage.

Borrow

Traditional Banks: rely on credit scores and underwriting models to price risk, absorbing the bad debt if a borrower defaults.

Neobanks: Generally cannot originate unsecured loans or credit cards because they lack a banking charter.

DefiBanks: Utilize a "whole-portfolio" approach, combining onchain collateral enforcement with offchain data (via cryptographic proofs). This allows them to serve "credit-invisible" users, such as immigrants or businesses with strong cash flow but no credit history.
