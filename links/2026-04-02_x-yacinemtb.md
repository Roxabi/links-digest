Neural networks are going to eat the software world. Not in the way that people expect - through the AI itself producing software, writing code that compiles down to instructions. Instead, the neural network will *be* the software. Predictors.

People are too focused on code producing language models. That's not what is going to replace human produced code. Have you noticed? How much you are relying on neural networks day by day?

Control systems will get replaced with predictors. Image encoders will get replaced with predictors. Search indexes, slowly becoming more neural over time. Recommendation systems - hand written features getting removed for the ever growing optimization of information delivery. Video codecs are hand-rolled today, but you want different ones for different sorts of videos. Neural video codecs. NVIDIA DLSS4 is just a stable diffusion LoRA filter. 3D rendering produced by NERFs.

All of the information you consume is served by non-interpretable bags of floats. The fraud predictor when you swipe your credit card multiple times a day? The spam call rejector. How much software, that used to be hand written?

Software 3.0, non interpretable information transformers, predictors, mystery bags of floats, living in a cradle of interpretable 2.0 instructions. Software is changing.

A neural network is a computer program. We have methods of mining the space of possible computer programs, to find one that solves any given task. Neural networks work, universally. Our methods for mining the space are good, and they are improving rapidly. If there is enough information in the input, you can find the relationship to the desired output.

Some software is too difficult to write. The world is too dynamic. There is too much space. The amount of compute, human or otherwise, necessary to produce an extremely compressed interpretable 2.0 style computer program is too great. The compression & interpretability itself trades off robustness, fuzziness, qualities that are often advantageous.

Traditional control systems do not have enough degrees of freedom. The rigor to compress them removes the magic that computers are capable of. Your eyeballs dart around when you drive, moving your fovea around. Green light says go. Car moving to my right. Mirror check. Your control system drives your state estimator, which drives your control system. How could you possibly write a computer program to do that?

Let go of interpretability.

In a world where the only constant is change, your method of producing computer programs needs to be quick. It needs to take you a minute. Writing 2.0 code is just too slow.

Hill climb everything. *Everything*
